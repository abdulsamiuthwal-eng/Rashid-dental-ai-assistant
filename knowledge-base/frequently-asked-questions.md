**No.** The AI assistant cannot and will not diagnose dental conditions. Dental diagnosis requires a physical examination by a qualified dentist. If you are experiencing dental pain or symptoms, please:

## Dental Health — General Information

> **Note:** The following information is general dental health guidance based on widely accepted dental guidelines. It is NOT specific advice for your individual situation.

### How often should I visit the dentist?

Most dental professionals recommend a check-up every 6 months. However, your dentist may recommend more or less frequent visits depending on your oral health history. Ask your dentist what interval is right for you.

### At what age should children first see a dentist?

Most dental associations recommend that children have their first dental visit by age 1 or when the first tooth appears, whichever comes first. Regular dental visits from an early age help establish good habits and catch issues early.

### What is gum disease?

Gum disease (periodontal disease) is an infection of the structures around the teeth, including the gums. In its early stage (gingivitis), it causes red, swollen gums that may bleed when brushed. In more advanced stages (periodontitis), it can damage the bone supporting the teeth. Regular brushing, flossing, and professional cleanings help prevent gum disease.

### What causes tooth sensitivity?

Common causes of tooth sensitivity include:
- Worn tooth enamel
- Exposed tooth roots (from gum recession)
- Cavities
- Cracked teeth
- Worn fillings
- Teeth grinding (bruxism)

If you experience persistent tooth sensitivity, please consult your dentist for a proper assessment.

### How can I prevent cavities?

General guidelines for cavity prevention:
- Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
- Floss once daily
- Limit sugary and acidic foods and drinks
- Drink fluoridated water where available
- Visit your dentist regularly for check-ups and professional cleanings

### What is root canal treatment?

Root canal treatment (endodontic therapy) is a procedure used to save a tooth that has a severely infected or damaged pulp (the inner soft tissue). The infected pulp is removed, the inside of the tooth is cleaned and shaped, and the tooth is sealed. Contrary to common perception, modern root canal treatment is typically no more uncomfortable than a filling. If you need root canal treatment, your dentist will explain the procedure in detail.

### How do I know if I need a filling?

Only a dentist can determine if you need a filling after a clinical examination. Common signs that may indicate a cavity include: tooth sensitivity to sweet, hot, or cold, visible holes or pits in teeth, tooth pain, or staining. However, many cavities have no symptoms in their early stages — which is why regular check-ups are important.
